Exotic Fruits

DISCOVER YOUR NEW FIVE-A-DAY!

Prickly Pear

Origin: Mexico

Description: The fruit of the Nopales cactus. The fruits can range in color from yellow and light green to orange, pink, and red, depending on the variety and ripeness.

Nutrition: High levels of vitamin C, B-family vitamins, magnesium, potassium, calcium, copper, and fiber.

Uses: Can be eaten raw or dry, and made into various jellies and jams, or alcoholic beverages like vodka.

Cherimoya

Origin: Ecuador, Colombia and Peru

Description: The fruit of a fast-growing evergreen tree that can grow up tp 30ft tall. The fruit is somewhat heart-shaped, 4 to 8 inches long and up to 4 inches wide. Cherimoya seeds are poisonous if crushed open.

Nutrition: An excellent source of vitamin C, vitamin B6, magnesium and fiber.

Uses: Can be eaten raw, often frozen and then eaten with a spoon like ice-cream. Often called "Custard Apple" due to the texture of the flesh.

Mangosteen

Origin: Sunda Islands and the Moluccas of Indonesia

Description: The fruit grows on a tropical evergreen tree. The outer rind of the fruit is thick and rubbery; inside are sweet, delicious, snow white segments similar in design to that of an orange.

Nutrition: High in antioxidants, magnesium and fiber.

Uses: Can be eaten raw or dry, and made into various jellies and jams, candies, or alcoholic beverages like vodka.

Star Fruit

Origin: Native to the Philippines, Indonesia, Malaysia, Vietnam, Nepal, India and Sri Lanka

Description: The fruit is about 5 to 15 centimetresin long. It usually has five prominent longitudinal ridges, but in rare instances it can have as few as four or as many as eight. The skin is a light to dark yellow when ripe.

Nutrition: High levels of vitamin C, B-family vitamins, magnesium, potassium, calcium, copper, and fiber.

Uses: The entire fruit is edible and can be eaten raw. They may also be used in cooking and can be made into relishes, preserves, and juices.

Rambutan

Origin: Malay-Indonesian region

Description: The fruit is a round to oval single-seeded berry, 3–6 cm in diameter. The leathery skin is reddish, and covered with fleshy pliable spines, hence the name, which means 'hairs'. Has a sweet, mildly acidic flavor very reminiscent of grapes.

Nutrition: High levels of vitamin C and B-family vitamins.

Uses: Can be eaten raw or dry, and made into various jellies and jams, candies, or alcoholic beverages like vodka.

Cloudberry

Origin: Northern Hemisphere from 78°N, south to about 55°N

Description: Native to cool temperate, alpine, arctic tundra and boreal forest, an amber-colored edible fruit similar to the raspberry or blackberry. It is not widely cultivated and is primarily a wild plant.

Nutrition: Very high in Vitamin C.

Uses: When eaten fresh, cloudberries have a distinctive tart taste, over-ripe, they have a creamy texture somewhat like yogurt. Can be made in to jams or for baking. In Nordic countries, traditional liqueurs such as Lakkalikööri (Finland) are made of cloudberry.

Dragon Fruit

Origin: Indigenous to Central America

Description: Obtained from several cactus species, its succulent stem provides the uniquely delicious fruit with moisture in the arid climates where it grows. They have pink, red or yellow skin and white or red flesh, but always the beginnings of overlaid leaves, similar to an artichoke, and small, black, edible seeds.

Nutrition: High levels of vitamin C, B-family vitamins, magnesium, potassium, calcium, copper, and fiber.

Uses: Usually eaten raw, can be used to make delicious cocktails.

Persimmon

Origin: Mexico

Description: Like the tomato, persimmons are not popularly considered to be berries, but in terms of botanical morphology, the fruit is in fact a berry. In color, the ripe fruit ranges from light yellow-orange to dark red-orange depending on the species and variety.

Nutrition: A rich source of Manganese, Iron, Vitamin C and dietry firbe.

Uses: Persimmons are eaten fresh, dried, raw, or cooked.

Buddha's Hand

Origin: Northeastern India or China

Description: A variety of citron that grows on a shrub or small tree with long, irregular branches covered in thorns. The fruit's fingers contain only the white part of the fruit and sometimes a small amount of acidic pulp, but many of them are completely juiceless and some are seedless.

Nutrition: High levels of vitamin C and Calcium. Has been used as a painkiller due to its anti-inflamatory properties.

Uses: Can be eaten raw, thinly shaved, candied or as flavouring in baking and alcoholic beverages like vodka.
